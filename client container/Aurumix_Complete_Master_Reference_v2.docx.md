## **1.1  The Core Idea**

Aurumix is a gold-backed hybrid digital asset ecosystem. It is not a cryptocurrency in the conventional sense, not a stablecoin, not a gold ETF, and not a standard tokenised commodity. It occupies a new category: a structured long-term investment programme in which every participant holds a proportional, auditable interest in physical gold — combined with a mathematically governed algorithmic growth engine — delivered through a digital token called the AURX.

The proposition is simple: take the world's most trusted store of value (physical gold) and combine it with the programmability of blockchain, a disciplined systematic investment structure, and a community-driven membership model, to create an asset that works for the long-term retail investor world-wide.

## **2.2  What the Gold Pool Is**

The 70% Gold Pool is exactly what it says: physical gold purchased and held on behalf of the aggregate investor pool. When an investor puts in $100, $70 is used to buy physical gold at the LBMA AM Fix price on that day. The gold is held in an LBMA-accredited vault (Brinks, Loomis, or Malca-Amit in Dubai). Aurumix does not hold gold directly — an independent custodian holds it. The gold is segregated from the custodian's own assets. It is insured for full replacement value. Quarterly verification, annual independent audit.

The gold pool grows with every new investor and every SIP payment. The mark-to-market value of the gold pool, divided by total tokens in circulation, establishes the Gold Floor Value per token — the absolute floor below which the token price can never fall.

| Gold Floor Value per token (GFV) \=   (Total Troy Ounces in Vault × Current LBMA AM Fix Price)   ÷ Total AURX Tokens Released to date This runs automatically at every Mining Event. Token price is always at or above this floor — never below. |

The 30% Algorithmic Pool is not a trading fund and not a speculative investment. ## **2.4  The Three Value Drivers of the Algo Pool**

| Driver | Mechanism | Behaviour |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| V1 — Gold Upside Mirroring | The algo pool mirrors gold price appreciation on the upside only | If gold rises 10%, V1 adds 10% to the algo pool. If gold is flat or negative, V1 \= 0\. The pool does not fall with gold. |
| V2 — Fixed Per-Event Top-Up | A fixed 0.25% increment added at every Mining Event unconditionally | Provides minimum baseline growth regardless of market. 24 events/yr \= \~6% annual minimum from this driver alone. |
| V3 — New Gold Ratio Premium | Appreciation formula to be built that shows appreciation from 100 percent going down to 8  percent in 25 years and further going down to 5 percent in next 25 years | V3 adds 100 % in the beginning, coming down by 20 % each year in first 3 years then from year 4 to year 25 should come down to 8 percent from 40% and further goes down to 5% by year 50\.  V3 in its pure form is a ratio — new gold added divided by gold already held. In the early years this ratio is naturally high. As the reserve grows, it naturally compresses. This is mathematically correct but practically insufficient. A pure ratio formula, left to itself, will compress faster than the ecosystem's growth targets require — because the gap between a small early reserve and a large mature reserve closes quicker than investor onboarding milestones are achieved. The raw ratio alone will not deliver the V3 contribution levels needed to keep the token price trajectory aligned with the growth phases Aurumix is designed around. To correct for this, a Target Alignment Multiplier is embedded into V3. This multiplier compares actual ecosystem growth against the planned investor and capital inflow targets for each phase — Year 1 to 3, Year 4 to 25, and Year 26 to 50\. When actual growth is tracking at or above target, the multiplier sits at 1 and V3 runs on its natural ratio. When actual growth is below target — fewer investors than planned, lower capital inflow than modelled — the multiplier lifts V3 modestly to compensate, ensuring the token price trajectory does not suffer disproportionately from a temporary shortfall in onboarding. When growth significantly exceeds targets, the multiplier applies a soft ceiling to prevent V3 from running too hot and creating an unsustainable premium that cannot be maintained in subsequent events. In practical terms this means V3 is always the product of three things working together — the raw new gold ratio reflecting real asset addition, the market sentiment signal from recent oversubscription behaviour, and the target alignment multiplier anchoring the output to the ecosystem's actual growth roadmap. None of these three inputs alone determines V3. Together they ensure that V3 delivers its intended contribution across each phase — high and energetic in Years 1 to 3 when the ecosystem is being established, gradually moderating through Years 4 to 25 as the reserve matures, and settling into a stable long-term contribution from Year 26 onward — without ever becoming a mechanical guarantee or a number that can be engineered in isolation from what is actually happening in the ecosystem at that moment.   |

The Algorithmic Growth Fee is the most nuanced fee. It is charged only on the incremental increase in the algo pool — never on the principal. Example: if the pool grows from $1,000 to $1,050, the fee is 5% of $50 \= $2.50. The original $1,000 base is protected by the 90% Floor rule.

The algo pool starts at 30% of total investment. Of this, 85% is the maximum that can be collected as fee over the investor's participation lifetime, with a permanent 15% floor that is never touched under any circumstance. The fee is charged at 5% on every incremental growth of the pool — never on the standing balance. Bank interest earned on the pool partially replenishes the balance and must be netted into every monthly calculation before fee is assessed. The fee rate begins at 5% and declines by approximately 0.001% after every 5 Mining Events, reaching a permanent floor of 2% around Year 13\. Fee collection automatically suspends the moment the pool balance touches the 15% permanent floor and resumes only if bank interest or subsequent growth pushes it above that floor again.  
The developer needs to run a month-by-month simulation that tracks pool balance, cumulative growth from V1+V2+V3, bank interest accrual, fee charged per event, cumulative fee collected, and remaining distance to the 15% floor — for each investor cohort separately, because each cohort enters at a different event with different V3 contribution levels. The key output the founding team needs from this simulation is the realistic timeline per cohort to reach meaningful cumulative fee collection, and the aggregate fee revenue curve across all cohorts as investor count scales from 500 in Year 1 to 60,000–100,000 by Year 10\. The growth rate assumptions built into V1+V2+V3 and the fee decline schedule must be calibrated together so that the aggregate fee revenue curve is commercially viable across all phases — early stage, growth stage, and maturity — without either variable being set in isolation from the other.

## **3.3  The Seven-Step Event Process**

Every Mining Event executes the following seven steps in sequence, as an atomic smart contract transaction:

* Step 1 — SCHEDULER FIRES: Time-based smart contract triggers the event. Schedule is defined at genesis and cannot be changed without multi-sig governance approval.

* Step 2 — GOLD PROOF-OF-CUSTODY: System pulls a signed attestation from the gold custodian API confirming weight, purity, and vault balance. Event cannot proceed if any discrepancy is detected.

* Step 3 — ALGORITHM POOL VALUATION: The Algorithm Pool Manager takes a NAV snapshot. Growth delta (current NAV minus previous snapshot) is calculated. This delta is the sole basis for fee calculation.

* Step 4 — TOKEN PRICE CALCULATION: Price \= GFV(t) \+ APV(t). Floor rule applied: Event Price \= Max(Calculated, Previous). Price can only be flat or higher.

* Step 5 — FEE DEDUCTION: Platform fee calculated on algorithm pool growth delta only. Collected to Fee Wallet before token allocation.

* Step 6 — TOKEN ALLOCATION: Each investor's allocation calculated by Priority Score formula. Tokens pushed to investor wallets in a single batch transaction.

* Step 7 — DASHBOARD & NOTIFICATION: Every investor's dashboard updated with new balance, portfolio value, and ICS score. Push notification sent. Event summary published to community feed.

| ⚠  COMPLIANCE NOTE: The entire event process must execute as an atomic transaction — all steps complete fully or fully roll back. Partial execution is a catastrophic failure scenario. The smart contract must be built with atomicity guarantee at its core. |

| SECTION 13 | The Agent Network — Distribution Architecture How licensed agents are structured, compensated, and governed |

## **13.1  What the Agent Network Is**

Aurumix operates a structured agent distribution network to reach investors at scale — particularly NRI, HNI, and diaspora investors in target markets who prefer guided, relationship-based investment onboarding over direct self-service. Agents are licensed intermediaries who help investors join the Aurumix ecosystem, manage their SIP setup, and act as the primary human touchpoint for their clients.

Agents are compensated from a fixed 15% share of the Algorithmic Growth Fee that Aurumix collects at each Mining Event. This 15% is paid out of the platform fee — it does not come from investor principal or from the gold pool. No investor pays anything additional because of the agent network. The agent commission is entirely Aurumix's cost of distribution.

| *The 15% agent commission is a share of the platform fee revenue only. It is not an additional charge to investors and does not affect the 70/30 capital split, the gold floor, or the algo pool balance in any way.* |
| :---- |

## **13.2  The Three-Level Hierarchy**

The agent network operates on a maximum depth of three levels. There is no limit on the number of agents at any level horizontally — the network can expand as wide as needed. But it can never go deeper than three levels. This hard limit on depth is a deliberate structural decision to prevent the network from taking on characteristics that could be classified as a multi-level marketing scheme under VARA regulations.

| Level | Title | Relationship | Description |
| :---: | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Level 1** | **Principal Agent** | Appointed by Aurumix | Directly contracted with Aurumix. Responsible for their own client portfolio and for any Level 2 agents they appoint beneath them. Maximum accountability tier. |
| **Level 2** | **Sub-Agent** | Appointed by Level 1 | Appointed and supervised by a Principal Agent. Manages their own client portfolio. May appoint Level 3 agents beneath them. Their performance is the responsibility of the Level 1 agent who appointed them. |
| **Level 3** | **Associate Agent** | Appointed by Level 2 | The deepest level permitted. Manages their own client portfolio only. Cannot appoint agents beneath them. The network ends here. |

| *Hard Rule: The agent hierarchy cannot exceed three levels under any circumstance. A Level 3 agent cannot create a Level 4\. Any attempt to do so is a breach of the Agent Agreement and results in immediate termination of the agent's contract. The Referral and Sub-Agent Contract (Contract 13 in Annexure 8.2A) enforces this rule on-chain — the smart contract will reject any referral registration that would create a fourth level.* |
| :---- |

## **13.3  The 15% Fee Split Model**

The 15% of platform fee revenue allocated to the agent network is distributed among the active levels in the chain above each investor. The split depends on how many levels are active in that investor's chain. The model is designed so that a solo Principal Agent who has no sub-agents below them captures the full 15% — there is no incentive to create an empty hierarchy.

| Network Configuration | Level 1 Gets | Level 2 Gets | Level 3 Gets |
| :---- | :---- | ----- | ----- |
| **Level 1 only (no agents below)** | **15% — Full share** | — | — |
| **Level 1 \+ Level 2 active** | **9% of fee** | **6% of fee** | — |
| **Level 1 \+ Level 2 \+ Level 3 active** | **4% of fee** | **5% of fee** | **6% of fee** |

The split percentages above are the recommended starting structure. The exact percentages are tunable through the Governance Contract and may be adjusted over time as the network matures. However, the total across all levels at any configuration must always equal exactly 15% — the platform's commitment to distribution cost is capped at this level regardless of network depth.

Commission is calculated per investor, per Mining Event. The system traces each investor's agent chain at the time of each event, identifies which levels are active, and distributes the 15% accordingly. An agent who becomes inactive (no client activity for a defined period) is temporarily removed from the commission chain for that event — their share flows up to the next active level above them.

## **13.4  What Agents Can and Cannot Do**

The agent relationship is strictly defined. Agents are distribution and onboarding intermediaries — they are not portfolio managers, they are not given custodial control over investor assets, and they cannot make unilateral decisions about an investor's holdings.

| Agent Permission | Detail | Status |
| :---- | :---- | :---: |

| View client portfolio summary and ledger | Agents can see a summary ledger of their clients — total invested, current portfolio value, ICS tier, SIP status, and event allocation history. Sufficient detail for agent oversight without exposing sensitive personal data beyond regulatory requirements. | **ALLOWED** |
