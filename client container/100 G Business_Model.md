## **4.1  The Core Concept**

Aurumix generates operating revenue from entry fees, annual gold custody fees, and credit facility fees. A defined portion of the total annual operating profit — between 15% and 20% — is distributed back to investors as a dividend. This distribution is not uniform. It is weighted by each investor's ICS score multiplied by their investment value — meaning the most committed, longest-tenure, highest-value investors receive the largest share.

This dividend is the mechanism that converts Aurumix from a passive gold storage product into an active wealth-building ecosystem. The investor who holds consistently, who builds their ICS score through disciplined participation, and who grows their gold holding over years, earns a proportional share of the business they helped build. That is not just a retention mechanic. It is a genuine alignment of interest between investor and platform.

The ICS-Weighted Score is the product of two things — the investor's ICS score and the dollar value of their total investment. This means neither commitment alone nor money alone determines the dividend. An investor with high ICS but small investment and an investor with large investment but low ICS both receive a moderate share. Only the investor who combines genuine long-term commitment with growing investment value reaches the top tier.

## **4.5  Why This Is the Most Powerful Retention Mechanism**

Lock-in prevents exit by contract. The dividend prevents exit by economics. An investor approaching their lock-in maturity who knows that their annual dividend share depends on their continuous ICS score has a powerful financial reason to renew their SIP rather than redeem — the moment they exit, their ICS score begins to decay, and with it their dividend entitlement. The dividend converts lock-in expiry from a potential exit point into a renewal moment.

And it compounds. An investor who has been in the ecosystem for 5 years, with a large gold holding and maximum ICS score, is in the top dividend tier. Their annual dividend is paid in USDT or added as gold grams. If added as gold grams, the holding grows faster than SIP contributions alone. ICS score reflects the larger holding. Dividend entitlement grows. The cycle is self-reinforcing — and unlike the old algorithm pool, it is self-reinforcing because real operating profit is being shared, not because a formula is booking gains that do not exist.
